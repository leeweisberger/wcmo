Alternate Dispute Resolution

Arbitration and Mediation are the chief methods of this process. The Elder Law Attorneys at Williams Caliri Miller & Otley, PC care about the rights, dignity and long-term financial, mental and physical health of our senior citizens. In fact, you can say we are quite passionate about the subject.

Williams Caliri Miller & Otley, PC can help guide you or your aging loved one through a myriad of elder law issues such as:

  • Selecting and financing of long-term care
  • Gaining access to public benefits
  • Estate planning and tax issues

Seniors have unique concerns and questions. Being elderly does not automatically equate to being incapacitated, but, when such issues do present themselves, good planning can be extremely valuable. It happens all too often that even well-meaning assistance can have disastrous results in this area of the law which is why consulting with a skilled elder law attorney is so important. One way of telling whether an attorney is skilled in elder law is to look for the CELA designation. What is a CELA? It stands for Certified Elder Law Attorney.

The National Elder Law Foundation (NELF) uses CELA as the gold standard of elder law services. Certified Elder Law Attorneys are required to pass an examination, practice elder law for at least five (5) years and undergo a peer review every five (5) years.

According to NELF, there are approximately 500 CELAs in the United States. Our Certified Elder Law Attorney, Jeffrey R. Bascelli, JD, has been a Certified Elder Law Attorney for over twenty-five (25) years and is one (1) of the original sixty-nine (69) Chartered Certified Elder Law Attorneys in the United States.

Contact Mr. Bascelli to guide you or a loved one through the complex issues that can impact the long-term health and security of senior citizens.
